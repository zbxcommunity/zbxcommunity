Thou shall not ask stupid questions
It is important to be as specific as possible with your questions, otherwise you will likely get ignored.

If you want us to help you, please help us and follow the following guidelines:
  • Describe the symptoms of your problem or bug carefully and clearly.
  • Describe the environment in which it occurs (machine, OS, application, whatever). Provide your vendor's distribution and release level (e.g.: “Fedora Core 7”, “Slackware 9.1”, etc.).
  • Describe the research you did to try and understand the problem before you asked the question.
  • Describe the diagnostic steps you took to try and pin down the problem yourself before you asked the question.
  • Describe any possibly relevant recent changes in your computer or software configuration.
If at all possible, provide a way to reproduce the problem in a controlled environment.

Some examples:
Smart:
  • I have an LLD rule and getting the following error: $SCREENSHOT. I tried already X Y Z and that does not work. Now i am stuck, who got any suggestions?
  • Trying to filter out $SERVICE and created this filter in my LLD rule: $SCREENSHOT but it does not work. Who can spot my mistake?
Not smart:
  • HELP! Zabbix does not work!
  • My Discovery rule does not work
  • Can we monitor $RANDOM-APPLICATION-NAME?
First search the internet for yourself, before coming here to ask questions

Zabbix has been in development since 2001, and is used by a lot of companies. As such, both the Zabbix Documentation as Google provide a wealth of information.

Chances are that your specific question has been asked before, has been documented and probably has been solved.

Nobody likes to repeat themselves, nobody likes to answer the same question over and over. Please read the manual first. Please Google your question first. If both render useless, feel free to ask for support, but ensure you include what you've found and why it didn't work.

Be explicit. "I have an error" is not informative

You want something from the community: support.

The least the community may expect from you, is to make it as easy as possible for us to help you. "I have an error" is as if you go to a doctor and say "I have pain".

Though that is an issue that can be solved, it cannot be solved without any further information. Same goes for Zabbix: an error is not informative. As such, please include as many details as possible.

Community support is not free consultancy. We won't do your homework, nor work assignment

We are good at spotting homework (or work assignment) questions; most of us have done them ourselves. Those questions are for you to work out, so that you will learn from the experience. It is OK to ask for hints, but not for entire solutions.

We get it, most of use didn't like homework either. But trust us, the experience you gain has value. Getting a complete solution from some random stranger on the internet, doesn't provide any experience and thus adds no value for you.

If you need help, make it as easy as possible for us to help you

We want to help you, really. We want to work the problem, and working the problem isn't helping you getting you to first tell us what the issue is. So, help us help you and provice as many details as possible, so we can help you with the problem.

A screenshot is not a question

Just sharing a screenshot wont get you any closer to getting help. We don't consider a screenshot as a question and thus most likely simply ignore you.

Use screenshots to support your question, to make clear what the issue is or to show how you've configured things, but make sure you add some text with what the issue is and how we can help you (ie: the question)

Be polite or be banned. There is no middle ground

We're here to help, in our own time - without getting paid. As such, we expect you to be polite - which isn't unreasonable to expect in any case. We can't be more explicit than this: be polite or be banned.

We provide support about Zabbix, not Grafana, basic linux, ...

Though most of use do have at least some knowledge of other products, solutions et cetera - the scope is Zabbix. Not Linux, not Grafana, not Mysql. We do understand those components interact with or are fundamental to Zabbix, so there is a thin line.

"How come I cannot update the Debian Zabbix packages" is acceptable if it's really Zabbix related. If its an issue with your Linux installation, please use other sources for support

"Why isn't Grafana updating $data from the Zabbix datasource" is on the line, so please ensure its a Zabbix issue and not an Grafana issue.

Be patient. SLA's don't apply in community support

Community support is provided "free of charge". As such, please be patient. We all have jobs and most likely other priorities. As such, there is no SLA or even an guarantee we'll reply to your question.

If all else fails, get paid support. There are people here who do consultancy and will give you the best service ever

If we as community cannot (or dont want to) answer your question, solve your issue OR cannot do so within a timeframe you require - please get paid support

Zabbix itself as well as many partners and freelance consultants provide support. Some of those partners/consultants participate in providing Community Support, some not.

If you require answers within an agreed timeframe, if you need an SLA, if you need any development work done, please use paid support.
